Methodology

The carbon app is a tool we have designed to help people measure and track their carbon emissions. To do this, the app is based on this methodology, or a set of steps, to provide an estimate of how much CO2 is being emitted through different types of transportation.

To use the carbon emission app, the users need to provide the necessary data and put it into the app. For example, is someone takes a car ride, the app will need to know how far they traveled, what type of car they were driving, and what kind of fuel the car uses. Once the app has collected this information, it uses formulas to estimate the amount of carbon emissions produced by the activity. The formulas is presented further down in this page.

  1. Total sum & presentation

    The carbon dioxide emissions produced by buses are organized in four categories. The carbon dioxide emission produced by diesel buses is 0.10231 grammes per kilometer. The carbon dioxide emission produced by CNG buses is 0.08 grammes per kilometer. The carbon dioxide emission produced by petrol buses is 0.10231 grammes per kilometer. If the buses do not use fossil fuel, their dioxide carbon emissions are 0 grammes per kilometer.
    To work out buses dioxide emissions, we use the next formula: \[CO^2 = {Cte + \sqrt{f(km)} \over t}.\]
    Where:

    • \(Cte\) is a constant, since the engine requires some fuel to initialize.
    • \(km\) are the kilometers.
    • \(f\) is a coefficient depending on the type of fuel (see paragraph above).
    • \(t\) is the temperature, since in winter the engines require less fuel.
